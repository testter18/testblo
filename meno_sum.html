Meno - Plato's Dialogue Summary

This summary is originally written couple months ago. I'm just publishing it now. As far as the style of this post goes, I won't make opinions whether or not I agree with the methodology or arguments. There's very little commentary.
Basically these are my notes.

-----

The dialogue starts instantly by Meno asking Socrates if virtue can be taught.

From here Socrates tries to get Meno to give a definition of virtue to progress in the inquiry, but instead Meno lists a bunch of virtues, not what virtue itself is.

Meno understands what Socrates is getting at and comes up with a definition for virtue:
"Rule well in a just way"

Socrates points out that the definition is (implicitly?) circular, because earlier Meno named justice as a virtue.
Therefore virtue is implicitly mentioned in the definition: "Rule well in a just way"

Circular definitions can't convey meaning

At some point Meno points that if you don't know what virtue is, you can't search for it- because you don't know what virtue is, you wouldn't be able to tell it even if you saw it.

The concept of Meno's paradox comes here, Socrates phrases the paradox this way:
"A man cannot enquire either about that which he knows, or about that which he does not know, for if he knows, he has no need to enquire: and if not, he cannot: for he does not know the very subject about which he is to enquire."

Here Socrates points out to the Theory of Recollection/Theory of Forms and argues that the soul knows what virtue is, and inquiry can point to what virtue itself is.

-----

To the question whether or not virtue can be taught:

Socrates argues that virtue cannot be taught because high-up Athenians haven't been able to teach virtue to their sons, despite being able to teach all kinds of other things.

If virtue could be taught, like for example shoemaking, there would be teachers teaching it. There's no teachers or pupils with virtue as a subject.

People also aren't born with virtue, and people can't recollect virtue, so soul doesn't contain virtue by this hypothesis according the Theory of Recollection.

Socrates doesn't claim to know where virtue comes from, but infers that virtue is a gift from gods.

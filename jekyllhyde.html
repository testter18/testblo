Strange Case of Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde - Completed

I finished reading "Strange Case of Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde" today, it was a good read. I stayed consistent and did read atleast a bit every day, even though sometimes it felt pretty hard to me-I'm proud of staying consistent and sticking to my plan.

Also, holy fuck grammar can be hard :D I had quite a bit of problems with understanding terms and following everything in the book. Nevertheless, this was a good challenge for me.

I especially liked the last chapter of Jekyll's statement on the case, and I fully get why Carl Jung would reference this book in "Man And His Symbols". I'm usually no fan of anything horror, but I overall enjoyed this, the reason being the concepts in the book and the more light feeling- for me.

I feel good about having read this classic and understanding the possible references in depth in possible future, if this ever comes up (Probably won't lol)(Maybe with Jung) Regardless, I feel good about it.

I might write more in-depth summary or analysis of the book in the future, the theme appeals to me and the metaphor to conscious/unconscious + human duality are interesting concepts. As for this post, this is just an update.

Today is Sunday, and tomorrow I'll very very likely start my last week at work, most likely I'll only work for two days.

I have a lot of plans where to focus then, when I have a lot of free-time. One thing that's for sure one of my goals that I'll start working from the start, is updating this site in terms of building it. There's a lot of things that are lacking. But I like that i've posted here regularly for the past month.

I'm a bit of conflicted on what to do after work ends now. Since I have many paths I can pursue, and one value I want to follow, is acting in a way, that if my behavior would be universalized, the society wouldn't collapse.

I think this value comes to the application of behavoir, for instance: if I would just fuck around and do nothing, that would go against this value. However, if I work on something I feel is meaningful, is productive and I think benefits society in some way, it wouldn't go against it. Even if the activities aren't straight work/have nothing to do with earning money

The next step is to figure how to solve this puzzle.
